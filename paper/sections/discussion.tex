Several limitations bound the claims. First, the comparison holds
hyperparameters matched where implementations share a forest backend, but the
baseline packages embody their own defaults elsewhere; results attribute
differences to design choices only where the ablation isolates them. Second,
the tasks are single imputations scored distributionally; we do not evaluate
multiple-imputation uncertainty propagation \citep{rubin1987multiple}, which
matters for inference from the imputed file rather than for the population
statistics microsimulation consumes. Third, the reweighting fragility family
is one formalization of downstream reweighting; other admissible families
would give different worst cases. Fourth, chained draws inherit the usual
compatibility caveats of sequential conditional specifications
