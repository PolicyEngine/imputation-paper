\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

Imputation sits upstream of every population statistic a microsimulation
model produces, and the files it produces are reweighted by consumers the
imputer never sees. We presented an estimator that treats survey weights as a
structural obligation rather than an option --- weighted bootstrap into the
forests, directly weighted regime gates, sequential chaining, and an
interface that refuses a silent unweighted fit --- and a population-view
harness that evaluates any candidate population against held-out survey
views with a strictly proper joint score, a reweighting-invariant coverage
axis, and a classifier two-sample test. Empirically, the interface rule is
the paper's largest single effect: on a survey whose design is informative,
the identical estimator fit without weights is six times worse on net-worth
marginal fit and 2.3 times worse on the population view, an error mode the
harness localizes to the weighted measure (repairable downstream) rather
than the support (not). Conditional methods preserve the demographics--wealth
joint that hot-deck matching's conditional-independence gluing attenuates,
zero-inflated components require gating that plain forests and linear
quantile models miss by 4 to 27 percentage points of zero mass, and marginal
metrics --- the field's default --- cannot see any of this, ranking an
unconditional marginal draw at or near the top throughout. The estimator is
available standalone as \populacefit{}; the harness, task configurations, and
every number in this paper regenerate from the accompanying repository.
