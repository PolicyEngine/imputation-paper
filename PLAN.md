# Experiment plan

The paper's claim structure: (1) survey imputation in common practice is
weight-blind in ways that distort population statistics; (2) populace-fit's
estimator — a regime-gated, sequentially-chained, weighted-bootstrap
quantile-regression forest — addresses this by construction; (3) each of its
three design choices earns its place empirically, against standard methods and
against ablations of itself. Every number in the manuscript is regenerated by
`imp sweep` + `imp figures` from committed configs; nothing is hand-edited.

## Method surface

The registry in `src/imputation_paper/methods.py` is the single source of
truth. Committed surface:

| Key | Category | What it isolates |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `populace_fit` | candidate | The full estimator (weighted bootstrap + regime gates + chaining) |
| `populace_fit_unweighted` | ablation | `weights="none"`: the weighted-bootstrap contribution |
| `populace_fit_unchained` | ablation | Targets fit independently: the chaining contribution |
| `plain_qrf` | ablation | Single ungated, unchained forest at matched hyperparameters: the gating contribution |
| `microimpute_qrf` | baseline | Standard QRF imputation |
| `microimpute_ols` | baseline | OLS with normal residual draws |
| `microimpute_quantreg` | baseline | Linear quantile regression |
| `microimpute_matching` | baseline | microimpute's matching imputer |
| `statmatch_hotdeck` | baseline | Classical nearest-neighbour-distance hot deck (py-statmatch) |
| `weighted_marginal` | harness | Dependency-free lower bound; CI only |

Adding or removing a method is a deliberate change to this table, not drift.

## Tasks

1. **SCF→CPS wealth** (salvaged design from the unpublished microimpute
   manuscript): impute household net worth from the Survey of Consumer
   Finances onto the CPS ASEC. Policy application: baseline SSI caseload and
   the SSI Savings Penalty Elimination Act — the exhibit for why imputation
   quality has policy consequences.
2. **Cross-survey structure** on CPS ASEC / SIPP / PSID holdouts: impute each
   survey's non-shared variables and score joint structure with PRDC coverage
   (the design microplex-evals used; re-run fresh here).
3. **Zero-inflated PUF-style components** (dividends, interest): the regime
   surface where gating and weighting matter most, plus the
   reweight-fragility stress test (the "landmine" diagnostic). Prior internal
   eCPS measurements of fragility exist but are NOT citable — they must be
   re-measured under this protocol.
4. **Cross-dataset sweep**: the six OpenML AutoML Benchmark regression
   datasets from the microimpute manuscript's appendix (e.g. `space_ga`,
   `brazilian_houses`; full list in that appendix), to test transfer beyond
   economic microdata.

## Protocol

- 80/20 donor/receiver holdout, repeated over 10 seeds.
- Splits are pure functions of the seed and **paired across methods**: every
  method sees the identical donor/receiver partition per seed
  (`experiments/holdout.py`).
- All metrics computed under survey weights; the unweighted ablation changes
  only the fit, never the scoring.
- Frozen inputs: each task's donor/receiver tables are built once, pinned
  (path + hash recorded in the run config), and shared across methods —
  the l0-paper's frozen-precalibration pattern.

## Metrics

Implemented (`experiments/metrics.py`): weighted pinball loss over a decile
grid; weighted Wasserstein-1 to the donor; weighted zero-share error.

Pending (stubs that raise, so nothing scores against them silently): PRDC
coverage (Naeem et al. 2020) for task 2; reweight-fragility (worst-case
single-record share of an aggregate under an admissible reweighting) for
task 3.

## Honesty rules

- No headline claims from diagnostic runs; a claim cites a committed run
  directory.
- Skipped cells are recorded (`skipped.csv`), never silently dropped.
- No fabricated or recalled numbers: prior eCPS landmine measurements and the
  microimpute manuscript's results are design references only until re-run
  here.
- Where the candidate does not win, the paper says so; the microimpute
  manuscript's own finding ("no method dominates universally") is part of the
  story, not a threat to it.

## Sequencing

1. populace-fit DataFrame front door — landed via
   [populace#290](https://github.com/PolicyEngine/populace/pull/290); the
   `populace_fit*` adapters wire against it.
2. Implement the five method adapters in `methods.py` (each verified against
   its package's real API, with a unit test per adapter under the `methods`
   extra).
3. Implement PRDC coverage + reweight-fragility with unit tests.
4. Data loaders for tasks 1–4 behind a `data` extra; pin artifacts.
5. Full sweeps into `runs/`; `imp figures`; write results/discussion.
6. PyPI publication of populace-frame/populace-fit and the standalone
   quickstart, so the paper's software section points at installable
   artifacts.
