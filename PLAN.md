# Experiment plan

The paper's claim structure: (1) survey imputation in common practice is
weight-blind in ways that distort population statistics; (2) populace-fit's
estimator — a regime-gated, sequentially-chained, weighted-bootstrap
quantile-regression forest — addresses this by construction; (3) each of its
three design choices earns its place empirically, against standard methods and
against ablations of itself; and (4) the right evaluation for any of this is
the **population-view harness**: one latent population, surveys as views of
it, candidates scored by projecting through each view against that survey's
holdout. Every number in the manuscript is regenerated by `imp sweep` /
`imp harness` + `imp figures` from committed configs; nothing is hand-edited.

## The population-view harness

Formal picture: a latent population distribution produces individuals; each
survey s is a view V_s = (variable subset, design, measurement idiom) and its
holdout is a weighted sample from V_s(population). A candidate weighted file
(any generator's) is scored per view, in the view's own variable space —
cross-survey consistency is never required, which is what makes the harness
usable even though surveys disagree with each other. Three axes per view
(`experiments/views.py`):

- **Energy distance** (weighted, strictly proper): hedging toward modal
  households is penalized by construction.
- **PRDC coverage** (weighted averages over support-geometry radii): the
  explicit anti-mode-collapse axis. Because it depends on the candidate only
  through its *support*, it is invariant to any reweighting of the candidate —
  the calibration-blind block of the scorecard.
- **Classifier two-sample AUC** (weighted, equal class mass): the omnibus
  distinguishability check (0.5 = indistinguishable).

Holdouts are never used upstream (fit or calibration), so the harness is the
stack's non-self-referential test surface. Relation to calibration: method
comparisons freeze weights (calibration out of the loop); running the harness
pre- vs post-calibration on one file measures what calibration did to the
joint — future work beyond this paper.

## Method surface

The registry in `src/imputation_paper/methods.py` is the single source of
truth. Committed surface (9 methods):

| Key | Category | What it isolates |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `populace_fit` | candidate | The full estimator (weighted bootstrap + regime gates + chaining) |
| `populace_fit_unweighted` | ablation | `weights="none"`: the weighted-bootstrap contribution |
| `populace_fit_unchained` | ablation | Targets fit independently: the chaining contribution |
| `plain_qrf` | ablation | Single ungated, unchained forest (microimpute QRF): the gating contribution |
| `microimpute_qrf` | baseline | Standard QRF imputation |
| `microimpute_ols` | baseline | OLS with normal residual draws |
| `microimpute_quantreg` | baseline | Linear quantile regression (per-quantile fits) |
| `statmatch_hotdeck` | baseline | Classical nearest-neighbour-distance hot deck (py-statmatch) |
| `weighted_marginal` | harness | Dependency-free lower bound; CI only |

Released microimpute (1.1.2) carries no Matching model (it exists only behind
an R dependency in the dev tree), so py-statmatch's NND hot deck is *the*
matching baseline. Adding or removing a method is a deliberate change to this
table, not drift.

## Tasks

1. **`scf_wealth`** — within-SCF imputation of `debt` (zero-inflated,
   nonnegative) and `networth` (sign-mixed) from demographics and income
   (SCF 2022 summary extract, implicate 1, `wgt` weights). The regime-gate and
   chaining surface, with ground truth.
2. **`imp harness` (SCF→CPS population view)** — each method fits wealth
   conditionals on an SCF donor split, imputes onto real CPS households, and
   the candidate (CPS demographics + imputed wealth, CPS household weights) is
   scored against the *held-out SCF* through the SCF view (shared predictors +
   wealth, jointly). `scf_sample_reference` — the donor split scored as a
   candidate — anchors the sampling-noise floor. Two profiles: **minimal**
   (6 shared predictors, 2 targets, ASEC 2025 receiver — the controlled
   instantiation) and **populace-scale** (10 shared predictors incl. edcl/
   race/housecl/lf, pooled ASEC 2023–2025 receiver mirroring the production
   spine, 4 chain-ordered targets with the exact balance-sheet identity
   networth = fin + nfin − debt). The scale profile tests whether the joint
   margins over matching widen with conditioning richness — the same
   empirical question as populace#297 (education as auxiliary).
3. **`cps_components`** — within-CPS imputation of interest and dividend
   income for adults, from the Census ASEC 2025 public-use files pulled
   directly from census.gov (never any processed or enhanced artifact: the
   paper's task inputs must not flow through PolicyEngine's own pipelines,
   and enhanced files embed the imputations under evaluation). The
   zero-inflation surface, plus the **reweight-fragility** stress test:
   worst-case single-record share of each component aggregate under bounded
   (κ=5) multiplicative reweighting, reported for every method next to the
   held-out truth's own fragility. ASEC amounts are topcoded, which truncates
   the extreme tail relative to the SCF.
4. **`openml_*`** — the six OpenML AutoML Benchmark regression datasets from
   the microimpute manuscript's appendix (space_ga, elevators,
   brazilian_houses, onlinenewspopularity, abalone, house_sales), uniform
   weights: transfer beyond economic microdata.

Full multi-view synthesis (CPS+SIPP+PSID candidates scored on all views at
once) is the populace release-gate use case and future work; PSID requires
registered access and is out of scope here.

## Protocol

- 80/20 donor/receiver holdout, repeated over 10 seeds; splits are pure
  functions of the seed and **paired across methods**.
- Oversized tasks are capped by a deterministic uniform subsample (seed 0,
  before splitting, shared by all methods/seeds) and the cap is recorded in
  `manifest.json`.
- The harness receiver sample is fixed once across methods and seeds, so seed
  variation isolates donor-split noise.
- All metrics computed under survey weights; the unweighted ablation changes
  only the fit, never the scoring.

## Metrics

All implemented in `experiments/metrics.py` with unit tests: weighted pinball
loss (decile grid), weighted Wasserstein-1, weighted zero-share error,
weighted energy distance, weighted PRDC (precision/recall/density/coverage),
weighted classifier two-sample AUC, and closed-form reweight fragility
(bounded multiplicative family, κ default 5).

## Honesty rules

- No headline claims from diagnostic runs; a claim cites a committed run
  directory.
- Skipped cells are recorded (`skipped.csv`), never silently dropped; row
  caps are recorded (`manifest.json`), never silent.
- No fabricated or recalled numbers: prior internal eCPS fragility
  measurements are design references only; every number in the paper comes
  from this repo's runs.
- Where the candidate does not win, the paper says so.
